# Pin — Design Context

## Users
Web agencies (5–50 people) and indie dev teams. Three personas inside each:
- **The reviewer** (designer / PM / client) — the non-dev who can't read a console but needs to flag UI issues on the live site
- **The implementer** (developer) — receives the auto-generated ticket
- **The owner** (agency lead) — buys the seat, cares about client trust + GDPR

Context of use: someone is staring at a live website, sees something off, and currently has to screenshot → annotate → Slack → describe → wait. Pin replaces that with a click.

## Brand Personality
**Three words:** opinionated, hand-crafted, technically literate.

Not generic SaaS. Not bro-tech. Not "playful illustrations." This is a tool made by people who care about typography and shipped pixels. The voice should sound like a designer talking to other designers — short sentences, real opinions, no marketing fluff.

Emotional goal: the visitor thinks "finally, someone built the thing I've been faking with screenshots."

## Aesthetic Direction
**Tone:** Editorial / manuscript. Warm paper, ink, and a single sharp red mark — like an editor's correction on a printed page. The metaphor of the product (a pin / mark on a live page) maps onto an aesthetic of editorial markup.

**References (spirit, not copy):** Linear's restraint, Vercel's typographic confidence, Are.na's editorial calm, the *NYT Magazine* feature-page rigor.

**Anti-references:** Jam.dev's QA-tool look. Generic SaaS hero with a gradient blob and a centered headline. "Modern startup" sans-serif with an icon-grid features section. Dark mode with cyan glow. Gradient text.

**Theme:** Light. Warm off-white background (paper). The product itself is consumed against live websites which are predominantly light, and the editorial metaphor demands light.

**Self-referential meta:** The marketing site uses Pin's own idiom — small numbered pin annotations appear in the margins commenting on the page itself. Proof of the product, executed on the page selling the product.

## Design Principles
1. **Show, don't list.** No icon-grid features section. Each feature gets its own composed scene.
2. **Type does the work.** Hierarchy through Bricolage Grotesque + scale + weight, not through colored cards.
3. **One accent, used sparingly.** Manuscript red (oklch ~58% 0.22 25) appears only on pins, key emphasis, and primary CTAs. The 60-30-10 rule, strict.
4. **Asymmetric and confident.** Left-aligned, generous margins, no centered hero. Break the grid for emphasis.
5. **The pin is the protagonist.** Every section reinforces the spatial-annotation metaphor. The product mark appears on the marketing page itself.
