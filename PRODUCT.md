# YouIn — Design Context

## Users
Web agencies (5-50 people), small product teams, and indie builders. Three personas inside each:
- **The reviewer** (designer / PM / client) — the non-dev who can't read a console but needs to flag UI issues on the live site
- **The implementer** (developer) — receives the spatial task or synced Linear/Jira issue
- **The owner** (agency lead / product lead) — buys the workspace, cares about review speed, context quality, and client trust

Context of use: someone is staring at a live web app, sees UI work, and currently has to screenshot, annotate, paste into Slack, describe the page, then wait for follow-up questions. YouIn replaces that with a spatial task attached to the actual interface.

## Positioning
YouIn is a spatial backlog for web apps.

Primary line: **Your product backlog, pinned to your actual UI.**

Do not position it as a broad "visual feedback tool." That category is crowded and pulls the product toward BugHerd / Marker.io comparisons. The sharper idea is Linear-style work mapped onto the live product: every bug, comment, and task lives exactly where it belongs.

## Brand Personality
**Three words:** opinionated, hand-crafted, technically literate.

Not generic SaaS. Not bro-tech. Not "playful illustrations." This is a tool made by people who care about typography and shipped pixels. The voice should sound like a designer talking to other designers — short sentences, real opinions, no marketing fluff.

Emotional goal: the visitor thinks "finally, someone built the thing I've been faking with screenshots, Slack threads, and half-readable tickets."

## Aesthetic Direction
**Tone:** Spatial OS. A quiet technical product interface with a precise red annotation layer. The metaphor of the product (a pin / mark on a live page) maps onto an aesthetic of product maps, inspection tools, and task surfaces.

**References (spirit, not copy):** Linear's restraint, Raycast's density, Figma Inspect's precision, Vercel's typographic confidence.

**Anti-references:** Jam.dev's QA-tool look. Generic SaaS hero with a gradient blob and a centered headline. Dark mode with cyan glow. Gradient text. Beige editorial everywhere.

**Theme:** Light by default. Clean technical neutrals with a small amount of warmth. The product itself is consumed against live websites which are predominantly light, and the red mark needs a quiet surface to stay precise.

**Self-referential meta:** The marketing site uses YouIn's own idiom — small numbered pin annotations appear in the margins commenting on the page itself. Proof of the product, executed on the page selling the product.

## Design Principles
1. **Show, don't list.** No icon-grid features section. Each feature gets its own composed scene.
2. **Type does the work.** Product UI uses system typography. Marketing can use Bricolage Grotesque for large headlines.
3. **One accent, used sparingly.** Manuscript red appears only on pins, key emphasis, active states, and primary CTAs. The 60-30-10 rule, strict.
4. **Structured and confident.** Product surfaces use predictable panes and dense scanning patterns. Marketing can stay more asymmetric.
5. **The pin is the protagonist.** Every section reinforces the spatial backlog metaphor. The product mark appears on the marketing page itself.
