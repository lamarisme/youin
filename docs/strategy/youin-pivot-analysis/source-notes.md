# Report source notes

## Reporting job

- Question: Which narrow market should Youin focus on or pivot toward where
  there is credible demand?
- Audience: Product stakeholder / founder.
- Decision: Choose one buyer, painful recurring workflow, offer, and short
  validation test before more product development.
- Scope: Existing Youin implementation, aggregate activity through July 17,
  2026, and current adjacent-market evidence.
- Comparison basis: Pain, willingness to pay, founder reachability, product fit,
  and competitive pressure.

## Required-structure mapping

- Title: `Youin’s best pivot`.
- Executive Summary: visible immediately after the title.
- Key findings with evidence: category evidence, product signal, and pivot table.
- Recommended next steps: the 14-day validation sprint.
- Further questions: explicit validation questions.
- Caveats and assumptions: explicit final section.

## Evidence and validation notes

- Aggregate database queries avoided names, emails, workspace names, URLs, and
  page contents.
- Totals reconcile: capture-kind rows sum to 57 marks; 44 closed of 57 is 77%;
  50 assigned of 57 is 88%; 48 marks in the largest workspace is 84%.
- The internal data is fresh but too small and concentrated to establish market
  demand, retention, or willingness to pay.
- Competitor counts and outcomes are self-reported. They establish category
  activity and pricing, not independent market share.
- Validation assessment: **Share with caveats**. The strategic recommendation is
  suitable for a customer-discovery sprint, not for a long roadmap commitment.

## Table and visual map

- `workflow_signals`: horizontal/category bar comparison of five aggregate
  workflow signals from the configured database. The counts share the same
  product-history window but can overlap and do not represent unique users.
- The pivot comparison is rendered as a Markdown table because its rows are
  qualitative analyst synthesis, not query-produced measures.
- No competitor market chart was included. User, team, company, and client
  counts use different definitions, so charting them together would create
  false quantitative precision.

## Open evidence gaps

- No verified paying-customer, revenue, churn, or acquisition data was found.
- Workspace identities were intentionally not inspected, so test/founder versus
  external-customer activity is unknown.
- Guest review-link completion, time-to-first-mark, repeat weekly use, and the
  fraction of prompts actually executed by an agent are not measured.
